Where did my love of programming come from?New

Posted on June 14th, 2021 by Abijah Ford
Web Dev Engineering Gen Z

I was born in 1999(era of the internet, social networking, and the smartphone) so that makes me A GenZer, but all my life I have been called an old soul. I think this comes from my ability to put myself in other people's shoes, and take on a conversation from their perspective of life. From my understanding, Generation Z tends to value individual perspectives and we avoid labels. But to me, there is more to life than being stuck in your understanding of this world.

People have so much to offer; stories about their experiences, trials and tribulations, and knowledge. Your eyes and ears are the passage to understanding life, take what you wish from a conversation, just know there is always more beneath the surface.

Up until I was around 10-12 I was generally a pretty shy kid, human interaction was anxiety-inducing for me as I would rather be in the comfort of my room. My bedroom was my little haven where I could retreat to, everything I owned had an assigned spot there as well as in my heart. It bothered me when I had company and they would jump around my room and knock things out of place, because 1. They should respect my space, 2. I didn’t want to clean up after them.

Some hobbies I had as a kid were dissecting objects and putting them back together, I did this with the following items; remote controllers, fans, toys, and even an old GameBoy Advance. It satisfied me knowing that I could take something apart piece by piece to then put it together and everything would work just fine, but as we all do, I ran into many problems that I had to learn from.

Filled with curiosity to the brim.

This leads me to my love of programming, computers fascinated me! Growing up in the age of the internet, social networking, and smartphones made me the man I am today. Instant messaging through AOL and designing my first MySpace page attributed to the love of the internet. Then came Minecraft, a wonderful survival game that was pretty much fully customizable as long as you knew how to HACK. At an early age, I assisted colleagues as an Administrator on multiple servers, to design, moderate, and deploy our creations for the world to enjoy.

From Minecraft came my love for internet programming, I took these same concepts of designing, bug fixing, and deploying my projects for the world to see and then hit the ground running.

Originally I was going to a community college for Computer Science, but as we all know Computer Science is such a broad field! So I had to decide on an area of interest to delve deep into, and that happened to be Internet Programming. I would always Control-Shift-I pages and change things to impress my parents even before going to college for it, but the changes made went away as soon the page is refreshed, so I had to find a way to make the changes stay.

I first started with HTML and CSS, dissected those languages until I could understand them thoroughly. Then I started learning PHP, JavaScript, and jQuery, and that’s when I knew there was no turning back. With those languages alone you can deploy a good-looking application, but as I furthered my knowledge in this field I found out about different styling frameworks(BootStrap), libraries(ReactJS), and databases.

My learning on the earth is certainly unfinished. This is my main driving force, I love to learn! If it wasn’t for all the objects I tore apart, not only in programming but in real life I’m not sure where I would be today. No one will understand the meaning of this life, but if you surround yourself with like-minded individuals that love to learn just as much as you do, you will most definitely get close to understanding your meaning in this life.

Abijah Ford - Proudly located in Roseville, Minnesota.
